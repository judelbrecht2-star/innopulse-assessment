Idea Management

Idea Management That Goes Beyond a Suggestion Box

Collecting ideas is easy. Turning the right ones into value is the hard part — and that is what a real idea management system is for.

Home › Services › Idea Management

Idea management is the structured process of capturing, evaluating, developing and advancing ideas so that the most valuable ones are resourced and delivered. Many organisations gather ideas enthusiastically and then let them die in a backlog. A working idea management system fixes the part that matters: deciding, transparently and quickly, which ideas move forward, who owns them, and how they connect to the innovation portfolio.

Why most idea schemes fail

The classic suggestion box fails for predictable reasons: no clear criteria for what makes a good idea, no owner to evaluate submissions, no feedback to contributors, and no link to funding or delivery. People quickly learn that ideas disappear into a void and stop contributing. The lesson is not that idea collection is pointless — it is that capture without a decision process is theatre.

The pipeline that actually works

  1. Capture. Make it easy to submit ideas against clear, strategy-linked challenges rather than a generic open box.
  2. Evaluate. Apply transparent criteria and named reviewers so decisions are fast and fair.
  3. Develop. Give promising ideas the time and small resources to be shaped and tested.
  4. Decide. Use clear gates to advance, hold or stop ideas — and tell contributors what happened.
  5. Deliver & learn. Move funded ideas into delivery and feed the results back into the system.

Idea management is part of a bigger system

Idea management is one component of a complete innovation management system. On its own it generates a pipeline; connected to strategy, governance and measurement, it becomes a reliable source of value. We design idea management that plugs into your wider innovation operating model, so the ideas you collect actually have somewhere to go.

Frequently asked questions

What is an idea management system?

It is the structured process &mdash; and often supporting software &mdash; for capturing ideas, evaluating them against clear criteria, developing the promising ones and deciding which to resource, with feedback to contributors throughout.

Do we need software for idea management?

Not to start. The decisive factors are clear criteria, named owners and a regular decision rhythm. Software helps at scale, but it cannot substitute for a working process.

How do we stop good ideas from being lost?

By replacing the open suggestion box with strategy-linked challenges, transparent evaluation, visible ownership and honest feedback, so contributors see that ideas lead somewhere.

How does idea management fit with ISO 56001?

Idea management supports the operation clause of an innovation management system. It is one part of the broader system ISO 56001 expects you to manage and improve.
